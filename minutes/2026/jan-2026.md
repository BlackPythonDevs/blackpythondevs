# Jan 2026 Leadership Meeting Overview

Meeting 6 Jan 2026

The meeting was led by Jay Miller, founder of Black Python Devs, and focused on community outreach, operational infrastructure, and long-term leadership transitions. Jay introduced several new leadership members, noting that the team has grown to seven people to better handle the organization's scale.


## Venezuela: Afro-Venezuelan Community Support

Jay initiated a discussion regarding the political situation in Venezuela and its impact on tech community support:

Demographic Recognition: The conversation highlighted that Afro-Venezuelan communities as well as Morenos, Mulatos, Mestizos were not officially recognized as a distinct demographic by their government until the mid-2000s.

Sanction Awareness: Jay emphasized the importance of monitoring the Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) sanction list to ensure the organization remains compliant while attempting to support tech communities in the region.

Mission over Politics: While BPD is a non-political non-profit, its mission is to support these marginalized people. However, the leadership cautioned against exposing the organization to government scrutiny or appearing to support foreign entities in a way that could lead to legal trouble.
  
Operations and Account Access
A significant portion of the meeting was dedicated to the organization’s "paper trail" and the tools used to maintain it.


## Q&A and Leadership Transition

Event Sponsorship: A question was raised regarding the process for sponsoring conferences. Jay explained that the typical process involves reviewing the budget (currently approximately $14,000–$15,000/year), verifying the event's needs, and getting approval from the GNOME Foundation (They review budget for funds)

Regional Communication: Leaders from the Africa and LATAM regions suggested using WhatsApp for more informal, accessible outreach, though Jay noted these must be carefully managed to maintain consistency and a record of communication.

Jay’s Transition: Jay announced a long-term plan to step down as Global Executor by the end of 2028, marking a five-year tenure. He emphasized that the current focus must be on documentation and creating a "playbook" so the next generation of leaders can step in seamlessly.

## State of BPD Reference -   

Jay referenced his recent "State of BPD" blog post, which outlines the organization's growth. In 2025, BPD supported 24 events, 14 of which were in Africa, and raised approximately $15,000.

Jay addressed the lack of gender diversity in the leadership team and has assured the team that, in the next election, great work will be put to be intentional about recommending potential leaders.
